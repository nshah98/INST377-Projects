About Me

Currently, I am a student at the University of Maryland pursuing a Bachelor's degree in Information Science with a concentration in Data Analytics along with a Minor in Statistics. I am interested in improving services for customers by using advanced analytics, big-data analytical tools, and creating/maintaining models.

Over the past year I have had the opportunity to work for the United States Department of Energy as a Junior Oracle Database Developer. As an Intern, I was a part of the Short-Term Energy Outlook sector, which includes a model of econometric regression equations and identities that data on the various components of the U.S. Energy together in order to develop consistent forecasts. My main responsibilities included developing new database objects like Sequences, Procedures, Functions, Triggers, and Views in the development and production environment to do further regression analysis within the Model.

Recently, I had the opportunity to intern for a Kelvin Inc. As a Data Scientist Intern, I was able to build and improve the Non-Arrival Response and the Ascent Velocity Models. These two models took in raw data and used it to improve the gas production from different wells. My main project included building an Elasticsearch API in order to improve filtering and sorting logger data from different entities.
